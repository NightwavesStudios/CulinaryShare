Ways To Cook An Egg


There are many ways you can cook an egg. Hard Boiled, Scrambled, Poached, Soft Scrambled, Sunny-Side Up… The list goes on and on. In this lesson, we are going to go over what we believe are the four most common and useful types: fried, scrambled, hard-boiled, and poached.



#1 Fried Eggs


View Fried Egg Recipe

Fried eggs are cooked in a skillet with a small amount of fat, resulting in crispy edges and a runny yolk when cooked sunny-side up or a firm yolk when cooked over-easy. They are delicious on their own or served over toast or alongside other breakfast staples.



#2 Scrambled Eggs (View Scrambled Egg Recipe)



Scrambled eggs are a classic breakfast favorite, made by whisking eggs and cooking them in a pan until they reach a creamy consistency. They are fluffy and light, often seasoned with salt, pepper, and sometimes milk or cream for added richness.



#3 Hard-Boiled Eggs (View Hard-Boiled Eggs)



Hard-boiled eggs are cooked in their shell in boiling water, or a special appliance called an “egg cooker” (Learn More About Egg Cookers in Appliances For Eggs) until the whites and yolks are firm. They are a convenient and portable snack or salad ingredient, offering a protein-packed option for meals on the go.



#4 Poached Eggs (View Poached Eggs)



Poached eggs are gently cooked in simmering water without their shells, creating a silky texture with a runny yolk when perfectly cooked. They are often served over toast, salads, or as a topping for dishes like eggs Benedict. Each egg preparation method offers a different culinary experience, catering to a wide range of tastes and preferences.



Each of these techniques of cooking eggs can be used in different scenarios. While scrambled eggs and fried eggs can be a hearty and quick breakfast, hard-boiled eggs are great for taking to-go or adding to a salad.
