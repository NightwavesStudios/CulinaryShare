Brownies


Allergies: Eggs


This brownie recipe was originally based on Beth Dooley and Lucia Watson’s Mrs. Macine’s Brownies recipe in their cookbook “Savoring the Seasons of the Northern Heartland”. Over the years, it was tweaked to include just the right amount of sweetness, crunch on the outside, and chewiness on the inside, before being passed down to CulinaryShare.

This is a great recipe for brownie lovers, and is easy for all skill levels to make. Furthermore, it is a very easy introduction into the world of baking! We hope you enjoy, and share what you make with us and your friends and family.


Prep Time: ~5 Minutes

Cook Time: 40 Minutes

Servings: Make Around 15-20 Brownies


Ingredients:

  • One Stick (8 Tbsp.) of Butter
  • Two Squares Chocolate (2 oz.)
  • ¾ Cups Sugar
  • 2 Eggs
  • ½ Tsp. Vanilla Extract
  • ¼ Cup Vinegar
  • ½ Tsp. Salt


Appliances:

  • Oven
  • Mixing Bowl
  • Whisk/Something You Can Mix Ingredients With




Prep Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees fahrenheit(163 celsius)
  2. Melt one stick of butter and the two squares of chocolate on the stove over low heat.
  3. When the chocolate and butter mixture in melted, turn off the stove, and cool the mixture
  4. While waiting for the mixture to cook, crack the eggs into a bowl, then beat (mix) together the eggs, sugar, and vanilla extract.
Cook Instructions:

  1. Transfer the mixture you have created into a baking tray/sheet/pan and put it in the oven. We used a small tray with circular holes that are used for muffins to make small bite-sized brownies.
  2. Cook for about 40 minutes, or until they look done.
  3. Cut & Take out how you please.
  4. Enjoy!
