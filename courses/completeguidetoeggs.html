Complete Guide To Eggs


Introduction
An overview of the entire course, including all of the recipes you will cook, things you will learn, and practice activities you will complete.
Lesson - How to Crack an Egg
This lesson teaches you two useful tricks to crack an egg easily, and without getting the shell in your food!
Lesson - Ways To Cook An Egg
This lesson teaches you the four most common ways to cook an egg, and it also shows and explains to you other ways to cook an egg besides the four main ways you will be learning to do later in this course.
Lesson - Appliances For Eggs
This lesson will teach you about all of the different appliances that are used to make eggs.
Practice - Match Egg Type with Appliance
This interactive quiz will test your knowledge that you just learned about the different appliances used to make eggs.
Recipe - Scrambled Eggs
This is the first of the four common egg recipes. With a little bit of practice, scrambled eggs can be easy to make, and can turn into the perfect breakfast when your feeling rushed on time!
Recipe - Fried Eggs
Another one of the four common egg recipes, fried eggs can also be an easy breakfast to make with a little bit of practice. If you don't get it right the first try, keep trying!
Recipe - Hard Boiled Eggs
The third of four common egg recipes, there are countless ways you can use hard boiled eggs. From salads to breakfasts, th possibilities are endless! Better yet, hard boiled eggs can last in the fridge for up to seven days!
Recipe - Poached Eggs
Lesson - How to Tell An Egg is Cooked
In some cases, it may be hard to determine if an egg is cooked or not. In this lesson, you will learn how to make sure what you are eating is safe, and if it isn't how to quickly fix the problem.
Practice - Identify Cooked & Uncooked Eggs
This interactive quiz will test you on the previous lesson: identifying if eggs are cooked or not. In this quiz, you will see a picture popup. From there, you have to determine if the egg is cooked or raw!
Lesson - Omelet Basics
Omelet's are a great, filling breakfast. This lesson will give you an overview and prepare you for making one.
Recipe - One Egg Everything Omelet
Tomatoes, Onions, Blueberries, Strawberries, Bacon... The possibilities are endless when it comes to making an Omelet. This lesson will tell you how to make a basic one egg Omelet, and from there you can be creative and add various fillings.
Lesson - How to Season Eggs
It turns out, plain eggs can taste quite bland. Too much seasoning however, and they'll taste too strong. This course will give an overview on how to season and put toppings on your eggs to make them taste that much better!
Practice - Identify Over & Under-seasoned Eggs
This interactive quiz will put what you just learned to the test. Analyze different pictures and determine if they're under-seasoned, over-seasoned, or just right.
Lesson - How to Plate Eggs
Even though an egg seems simple, when preparing a nice meal, it can still be important (or impressive) to plate your dish correctly. This lesson will show you all of the ways that you can plate your egg right before serving it!
Practice - Good or Bad Plating?
This interactive quiz will test you on what you just learned. Study different pictures, and pick wether you think they are plated correctly or incorrectly.
